About the team

The Indiana Pacers first joined the NBA in the season ranging between 1976-1977. The team struggled when they first began playing. In their first 13 seasons, they had only one winning season to show for their hard work and dedication. In the early 1990's the Pacer's had a switch in their luck and started to become one of the Eastern Conferences Top Clubs! Between the years 1994-2000, they made it into the conference finals 5 times. They also won two Central Division titles and advanced to the NBA finals in the year 2000.

Indiana has long been a part of the basketball fandom. There is a ton of support for high school teams and for Indiana University Hoosiers. This especially occured after their success in the 1970s as well as 1980s under the coach, Bob Knight. It all began in 1967 when the franchise began as a charter member of the American Basketball Association, and lives on today in the National Basketball Assocation.

About the Fieldhouse

Bankers Life Fieldhouse is an indoor arena located in Downtown Indianapolis, Indiana, United States. It opened in November 1999 to replace Market Square Arena. The arena is the home of the Indiana Pacers of the National Basketball Association and the Indiana Fever of the Women's National Basketball Association.
